Nick Vujicic

The Inspirational One

A brief summary of Nick Vujicic's journey

Early life

  • Nick Vujicic was born on December 4, 1982, in Melbourne, Australia, with tetra-amelia syndrome, a rare condition that caused the absence of all his limbs. His early years were marked by significant physical and emotional challenges.
  • He faced the difficulties of feeling different, experiencing loneliness, isolation, and enduring bullying due to his unique condition.
  • However, despite these adversities, Nick's spirit proved to be resilient and determined.
  • With the unwavering support of his family, he developed various skills to adapt and accomplish tasks using his unique abilities.

Career

  • Nick’s career as a speaker started at the age of seventeen.
  • He started an initiative in Social Emotional Learning (SEL) Curriculum, called “Attitude Is Altitude.” Under this curriculum, he teaches students about positive changes they need to inculcate in their lives and communities.
  • He is the founder of the non-profit ministry “Life Without Limbs” with its headquarters in Agoura Hills, California.
  • Vujicic released a DVD of a documentary movie named “Life’s Greater Purpose” which talks about Nick Vujicic’s biography, how he learned to use his mind and body to the fullest.

Achievements

  • In 1990, Vujicic's determination and courage impressed the world, and he was felicitated with the 'Australian Young Citizen Award'.
  • He was one of the contenders for the 'Young Australian of the Year Award' in the year 2005.
  • In 2010, he won the 'Best Actor in Short Film' award at the 'Method Fest Independent Film Festival' for his performance in the role of Will, from the movie 'The Butterfly Circus'.
